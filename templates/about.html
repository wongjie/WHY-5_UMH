About Shariah-Compliant Criteria

There are 2 main benchmarks used to determine whether a company is Shariah-Compliant:

1. Business Activities Benchmarks

1.1 The 5% Benchmark

The revenue earned from the following activities should not exceed 5% of the company's total revenue.
- conventianal banking and lending
- conventional insurance
- gambling
- liquor and liquor-related activities
- non-halal food and beverages
- tobacco and tobacco-related activities
- other activities deemed non-compliant according to Shariah principles as determined by the SAC

1.2 The 20% Benchmark

The renvenue earned from the following activities should not exceeed 20% of the company's total revenue.
- share trading
- stockbroking business
- rental received from Shariah non-compliant activities
- other activities deemed non-compliant according to Shariah principles as determined by the SAC

2. Financial Ratio Benchmarks

2.1 Cash / Total Asset

The amount of cash must not be more than 33% compared to the company's total assets excluding:
- cash placed in Islamic Accounts
- cash placed in Islamic Instruments

2.2 Debt / Total Asset

The amount of debt must not be more than 33% compared to the company's total assets excluding:
- Islamic Financing
- Sukuk
